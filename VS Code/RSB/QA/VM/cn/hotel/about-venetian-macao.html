Armillary Shpere

Location: Reception Area

A replica of an Armillary Sphere, which was used for centuries to study the sky and the celestial system, resides in The Venetian Lobby. Surrounding the Armillary Sphere is a fountain into which tourists toss coins for good luck.

Archangel Michael

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

Shown with sword drawn, Archangel Michael is one of many statues carved in high relief on the Doge’s Palace.

Archangel Gabriel

Location: The Campanile Tower (Outdoor Lagoon Area)

Shown standing atop the Campanile Tower.

Entryway Colonnade

Location: Reception Area

Taking you into the heart of the hotel from the Reception Area is the striking Entryway Colonnade, a grand colonnade of columns interspersed with images of famous Venetian sculptures. The ceiling of the enirvway colonnade is adorned with four stunning reproductions of famous frescoes bv Tiepolo, that depict the coronation of dignitaries and various gods paying homage.

Doge's Palace

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

Doges Palace, the seat of the Venetian government for centuries and home of the Doge, law courts, the city’s civil administration and the city’s prison. Adorning the Doge’s Palace are statues of historical, biblical, and mythological icons, including the winged lion of St. Mark the Evangelist, David, Mars, Hercules, Neptune, and the Lady of Justice.

Four Heroic Episodes

Location: Reception Area

Giambattista Tiepolo’s “Four Heroic Episodes,” painted around 1725 for Palazzo Sandi, can be seen on the Lobby Dome over the Armillary Sphere fountain. The episode nearest the concierge desk depicts Orpheus conducting Euridice out of Hades with his beautiful music.

CA'd' Oro

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

One of the finest examples of Venetian Gothic architecture, the Ca’ d’Oro or ‘House of Gold’, designed to be the most magnificent palace in the city, the façade was adorned in ultramarine, gold leaf and vermilion. We are confident that our own recreation here in Macao does justice to the original.

Campanile Tower

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

"As on the original, the Campanile Tower at The Venetian Macao is topped by a weathervane of the Angel Gabriel and the tower is adorned with various statues. The original tower received its share of historic visitors, including Galileo, Goethe, and Emperor Frederick III of the Holy Roman Empire, who is said to have ridden his horse up the tower in 1452."

Relief Cartouche On Contarini-Fasan Palace

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

This coat of arms is displayed on brick outside the Contarini-Fasan Palace.

St. Mark Lion

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

"Shown on the St. Mark’s Clock Tower, the lion represents the reincarnation of St. Mark."

St Mark's Square & Clock Tower

Location: Shoppes at Venetian

Within The Grand Canal Shoppes is our own tribute to the famous St. Mark’s Square with a central piazza area surrounded by buildings in the same ornate architecture to be found in Venice and including another reproduction of the city’s Clock Tower, complete with ringing bell that, just as for centuries in Venice, will help our own visitors keep track of time.

The Apotheosis of Venice

Location: The great hall (Shoppes at Venetian)

Painted by Paolo Veronese in 1585, this work can be seen on the ceiling of the Doge’s Great Room at the entrance to The Grand Canal Shoppes. Best known for the rich color and interweaving compositions he learned from Titian, Veronese – along with Tintoretto – decorated the chambers of the Doge’s Palace in Venice, and partially supplanted Titian as the official painter of the city.

The Bridge of Sighs

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

Connecting the interrogation rooms of the palace to Venice’s old prisons is the Bridge of Sighs. Designed by Antoni Contino, whose uncle Antonio da Ponte designed the Rialto Bridge, The Bridge of Sighs was built in the 16th century. The name is an invention of Romantic literature; legend says that from this bridge one could hear the sighs of the condemned as they were being led to the offices of the feared State lnquisitors.

The Clock Tower

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

Any recreation of Venice simply must feature the famous Clock Tower and as you will see the Venice of Macao is faithful to this. Originally built in the late 15th century, the richly decorated Torre dell’ Orologio is one of Venice’s most famous landmarks.

Rialto bridge

Location: Outdoor Lagoon Area

As the oldest bridge spanning Venice’s Grand Canal. Local legend says that lovers will be assured eternal love if they kiss on a gondola at sunset under the bridge and today, the Rialto Bridge is one of Venice’s busiest spots for tourists and locals alike.


 

Brand Story


More than a decade ago, Sheldon G. Adelson, chairman and CEO of Las Vegas Sands, had a vision that would transform Macao into one of the world’s major business, leisure and family destinations with a diversity of attractions and facilities that draw tens of millions of visitors each year.

The Venetian Macao became the first proof that the vision could be realised. Situated on the Cotai Strip, The Venetian Macao is a modern marvel in a city dotted with centuries-old Chinese and Portuguese architecture and UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Nothing like it had ever been seen in Asia. When The Venetian Macao opened on August 28, 2007, it was the third largest building in the world and the biggest single structure in Asia. Measuring 980,000 square metres, The Venetian Macao can hold the entire area of Bejing’s Forbidden City and still have room to spare.

Before Macao, Adelson had already had a long business career in the United States, beginning as a schoolboy entrepreneur with a newspaper vending business on the streets of his native Boston. He founded Las Vegas Sands in 1988 to build and manage hotels, including The Venetian Las Vegas, complete with a convention and exhibition space in one of the glitziest cities on Earth.

The connection with Venice is a romantic one; Adelson had honeymooned in the Italian city with his wife, Dr Miriam Adelson. The ornate architecture, rich cultural heritage, exquisite cuisine, the picturesque bridges and piazzas, and the gondolas gliding serenely along the city’s ancient canals captivated the couple. Adelson believed that hundreds of millions of others would be similarly enchanted if only the charms and grandeur of Venice could be brought to them.
